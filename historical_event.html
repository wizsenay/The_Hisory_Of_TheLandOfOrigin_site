Historical event of Ethiopia

Here are some of the most important Historical event in Ethiopia:

Battle of Adwa (1896):
: Ethiopian forces, led by Emperor Menelik II, defeated the Italian army — a landmark victory for African resistance against colonialism.
Founding of Axumite Kingdom :
One of the great ancient civilizations of Africa, Axum was known for trade, monumental obelisks, and early adoption of Christianity..
Introduction of Christianity:
Christianity became the state religion under King Ezana of Axum, making Ethiopia one of the first Christian nations.
Zagwe Dynasty and Construction of Lalibela Churches:
Zagwe Dynasty and Construction of Lalibela Churches (12th century) Note: King Lalibela built 11 rock-hewn churches, now UNESCO World Heritage Sites and important religious symbols.
Reign of Emperor Haile Selassie:
Reign of Emperor Haile Selassie (1930–1974) Note: A key modernizing figure and symbol of African unity, he represented Ethiopia in the League of Nations and resisted fascism.
Italian Invasion and Occupation (1935–1941): Known for its castles and palaces from the 17th century, often called the “Camelot of Africa” for its impressive royal enclosure.
Italian Invasion and Occupation (1935–1941) Note: Mussolini's forces occupied Ethiopia, but resistance led to eventual liberation with the help of Allied forces.
Liberation of Ethiopia:
Liberation of Ethiopia (1941) Note: Emperor Haile Selassie returned to power after British and Ethiopian forces defeated the Italians in World War II.
1974 Ethiopian Revolution:
1974 Ethiopian Revolution Note: A Marxist military group known as the Derg overthrew Haile Selassie, ending the monarchy and starting a socialist regime.
Red Terror :
Red Terror (1977–1978) Note: A violent political campaign by the Derg under Mengistu Haile Mariam that led to mass arrests, torture, and executions.
1984–1985 Famine:
1984–1985 Famine. Note: A devastating famine affected millions, drawing international attention and aid, but also exposing political mismanagement..
The Fall of the Derg:
Fall of the Derg (1991) Note: The Ethiopian People's Revolutionary Democratic Front (EPRDF) overthrew the Derg regime, leading to a new government.
Establishment of Federal Democratic Republic:
Establishment of Federal Democratic Republic (1995) Note: A new constitution created a federal system based on ethnicity, with regional autonomy and multiparty politics..
Eritrean-Ethiopian War:
Eritrean-Ethiopian War (1998–2000) Note: A bloody border conflict between Ethiopia and its former province Eritrea, leading to tens of thousands of casualties.
Nobel Peace Prize for PM Abiy Ahmed
Nobel Peace Prize for PM Abiy Ahmed (2019) Note: Prime Minister Abiy received the Nobel Peace Prize for ending the 20-year conflict with Eritrea and initiating reforms.
Northern Ethiopia Conflict:
Northern Ethiopia Conflict (2020–2022) Note: A devastating war broke out between the federal government and the Tigray People’s Liberation Front (TPLF), causing humanitarian crises.
