In the 17th and 18th centuries

In the 16th century, Europeans brought new ideas about war, religion and other things to Ethiopia! Their interest in Ethiopia was aroused; The great victory achieved by the Portuguese fighting the Muslims and helping the righteous John became the legacy of the Christian civilization of Ethiopia. The attempt of the Roman Catholics was also rejected. Due to the interest of the Ethiopians in Europe, diplomatic relations with the Portuguese and others began. In this way, Ethiopia and Europe could get to know each other better.

In the middle of the 17th century, Europe's interest in Ethiopia was high, but since it was a period of internecine warfare, it was necessary to cut off relations with other countries that weakened the country.

There was a lot of violence when Zedengal died. Among the many who wanted to be the king, the three main ones, 1st, their nephew Zedangal, 2nd son, Jacob 1, and 3rd nephew, Susnyus, wanted to inherit the king's throne. But the queen and the nobles wanted Jacob to be the king's son.

During this period, Paisa, a Spanish Catholic came to Ethiopia. "This man had a mission station in Axum with a school where the children of great people were educated. He was able to convert many people to the Catholic religion. Because he helped the Catholics, the Abubunu and the Orthodox Church, as well as Zelasene princes, cooperated and chased the king with a sword. When they failed to reach him and died, Zeslase was enough to restore Jacob in his place.
