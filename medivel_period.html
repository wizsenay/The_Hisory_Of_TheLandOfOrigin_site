Lalibela

The city of Lalibela is an amazing place nestled high in the mountains. Nowhere else in Africa, except in very few places on other continents, is the type of Lalibela church building found. It is not a stone on top of a stone, but the roof is carved from the earth to the bottom of a rock. When this is done, a wide area is chosen first; Then a trench is dug around it and a big rock is left in the middle. Next, it will be completely hollowed out until the stone columns that seem to hold the roof are left.

Among the buildings built in this way, medhani'alem church is the biggest one, which the length is 33, the side is 22 and the height is 11 meters. And 28 columns have inside of it. A'mde tsion (1037 to 1337)

A'mde tsion was the son of Arad and was also known as King Gebre Meskel. Yakuno Amlak was the founder of the Solomons, and Amde Tsion was the founder of the greatness of the kingdom. That is, he expanded the border of the empire by pushing it from the Islamic sultans in the south to Awash and to Gojam and Begemdir Shewa and Dunkel. Apart from the great war, King Amde Tsion was a king who helped the people by improving the kingdom and leading with good capacity and good administration. At the time, there were many churches and monasteries were built. Wherever he defeated the Muslims, many of them returned to their homeland. Many books were written. Much literary growth and demand was created. The news of the wars fought by the Column of Tsion includes the wars it fought against the Muslims.
